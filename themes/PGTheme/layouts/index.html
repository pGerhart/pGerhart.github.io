I am a doctoral student at the Privacy Enhancing Technologies research unit at TU Wien in Austria, under the supervision of Dominique Schröder.

Research

My research focuses on the design and analysis of advanced cryptographic protocols, in particular threshold and adaptor signatures, partially-oblivious pseudorandom functions (PoPRFs), and password-based cryptography. I am especially interested in achieving strong security guarantees in challenging settings, and in developing constructions that are both theoretically sound and practically efficient.

Education

I am a doctoral student in Cryptography at TU Wien, supervised by Dominique Schröder. I received an M.Sc. in Computer Science from FAU Erlangen-Nürnberg (2020–2022), where I specialized in security and cryptography. I also hold a B.Sc. in Mathematics from the University of Bonn (2017–2020), focusing on lower-dimensional algebraic topology, in particular Riemann surfaces and 3-manifold topology.
